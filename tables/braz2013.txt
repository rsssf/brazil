#### Copa do Brasil  ‹§cb›


Obs.: From 2012 to 2013, CBF changed the classification criteria to Copa do Brasil.
          Now, Copa do Brasil will have 86 participants, being:

- The 6 Brazilian participants of Taça Libertadores da América (which will enter in
  Copa do Brasil only in 1/8 Finals);
- 70 clubs qualified from the state competitions, being:
    - 5 from each state ranked from 1st to 2nd in CBF ranking;
    - 4 from each state ranked from 3rd to 5th in CBF ranking;
    - 3 from each state ranked from 6th to 14th in CBF ranking;
    - 2 from each state ranked from 15th to 22nd in CBF ranking;
    - 1 from each state ranked from 23rd to 27th in CBF ranking;
- 10 best placed clubs in CBF ranking (excepting those 76 above).

In the years when Brazil have only 5 clubs in Taça Libertadores (i.e. when previous
year's winners were not a Brazilian club) the 6th team to receive a bye to 1/8 Finals
will be the best one placed in CBF ranking among those 80 non-Libertadores clubs. In
this case, this team will be replaced by other in the 1st Round.

CBF also changed his ranking system which now counts only the competitions of the
last five years (i.e. from 2008 to 2012).
