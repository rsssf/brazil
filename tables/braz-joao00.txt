## Brazil 2000 - Copa João Havelange


After the end of the 1999 championship, Gama contested in a common justice court its
relegation, caused by the decision of the sports justice court to award Botafogo-RJ and
Internacional victories over São Paulo.  Common justice ruled in favour of the team from
Brasília, which caused the Clube dos 13 (association of the Brazilian largest clubs) to
organise their own championship.  However, common justice again ruled in favour of Gama
and forced the Clube dos 13 to include the latter in their tournament.  CBF, who at first
had given up on the intention of organising the national championship, decided to ratify
the tournament, although it did not obey the promotion and relegation rules of 1999, and
in which the allocation of the teams in the four modules was based on unpublished criteria.
However, the common justice decision was respected and Gama was included in the tournament,
in spite of a ban by FIFA forbidding teams to play Gama.

The tournament is divided in four modules (Blue, Yellow, Green and White).  The final phase
will have 12 teams from the Blue module, 3 from the Yellow and 1 from the Green and White.
There will be no relegation or promotion this year.
